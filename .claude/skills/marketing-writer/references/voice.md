# Marketing Writer — Brand Voice Guide

This file defines the tone, linguistic style, and formatting rules the skill must apply to every output.

---

## 🎯 Core Principles

1. **Casual and direct** — talk like a friend who knows their stuff.
2. **Zero hype** — no corporate buzzwords or filler adjectives.
3. **Concrete > abstract** — show what happens, not how great it is.
4. **Empathetic** — speak to real pains developers and builders feel.
5. **Readable at a glance** — short sentences, clear formatting, no jargon walls.

---

## ✅ Do

- Use second person (“you”, “your team”).
- Start with a relatable pain, then show the fix.
- Use active verbs: _build, ship, save, skip, fix_.
- Prefer examples: _“Before: 10 steps. After: 1 click.”_
- Keep paragraphs under 3 sentences.
- Use bullet lists for clarity.
- Write numbers plainly: _“2× faster”_, _“under a minute”_.

---

## 🚫 Don’t

- Say “innovative”, “cutting-edge”, “best-in-class”.
- Promise magic: if it’s a 20% improvement, say that.
- Overuse emojis or exclamation points.
- Speak in abstractions like _“enhances productivity”_.
- Write more than 2 sentences without a line break.

---

## 🪄 Formatting Rules

- **Headers:** Sentence-case, never all-caps.
- **Bullets:** Simple hyphens or asterisks; no decorative icons.
- **Links:** Plain markdown `[text](url)`.
- **CTA:** Short and friendly, e.g. “Try it free → link”.
- **Emphasis:** Use `**bold**` for feature names and value props.
- **Voice consistency:** Always sound like one helpful teammate, not marketing automation.

---

## 🧩 Tone Variations

| Mode               | Use When                                       | Guidance                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **default**        | Everyday posts and features                    | Balanced, conversational               |
| **friendlier**     | Community, newsletter, or early-access updates | Add warmth, small emojis allowed       |
| **more technical** | Dev audiences or docs launches                 | Add technical context, skip fluff      |
| **short & punchy** | Social media hooks                             | Max 10 words per sentence, high energy |

---

## 🔍 Examples

**Before (corporate):**

> Our cutting-edge integration empowers teams to seamlessly leverage cross-platform synergies.

**After (our voice):**

> Connect it once — it just works everywhere you already are.

---

Following this guide ensures every landing page, tweet, and email sounds authentically like _you_.
